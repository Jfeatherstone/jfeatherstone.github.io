Physics

I am an undergraduate student studying physics at North Carolina State University. I am primarily involved in Karen Daniels's lab investigating granular physics and soft matter, though I have also recently started working with Vladimir Skokov on color glass condensate simulations. Per #SketchYourScience (specifically, Irene Crisologo) I also draw sketches of various scientific concepts to include in my research presentations (which is what you'll see throughout the site)!

Programming

On top of my work as a physicist, I often work on side projects creating useful applications like a todo list, color blindness simulator, and quantum variational solver. These projects span several languages, including Python, C++, Java, Go, and Matlab, and I've also written a few tutorials on how to make specific programs.

Mentoring

I have worked as a student mentor in the Honors and Scholars Village at NC State for two years now, during which I've hosted tutoring sessions for physics, outreach events for high schoolers, and streamlined communication methods within our team. I was named the NC State Mentor of the year in 2020, and was accepted to present at the National Collegiate Honors Council, though this ended up being canceled due to the pandemic.
